About the Barangay

Barangay Dinaga is within the poblacion area where you can locate the Municipal Hall, Elementary School, Canaman Parish Church, Public Cementery and Business Establishment. Where most of the poeple are employed in public and private establishment. However still a large part of the land area is agricultural but not full culminated due to drowned areas.

Barangay Dinaga is one of the biggest Barangay in the Municipality of Canaman in the province of Camarines Sur. it is located East of Barangay Baras and poblacion area. It has a land area of 58 hectares. It composed of six(6) zones namely: zone1, zone2, zone3, zone4, zone5, zone6.

Barangay Dinaga is characterized by lowland and suited for planting rice and the main source of livelihood of the poeple are farming and livestock. The Dialect or Language spoken are Bicol and English.

Agriculture:

One of the major source of income in Barangay Dinaga is rice farming. Farmers sells their produce either raw "palays" or rice to traders at nearby barangay or outside the municipality. Aside from rice farming, some raised pigs, chicken and other livestock in their backyard to augment their income and few are engaging in vegetable production as additional source of income as most of vegetables planted are just their own consumption.

Population:

Based on the Barangay Rapid Assessment Survey of 2007, Barangay Dinaga has an estimated population of 1,541, distributed to six zones. it has 315 households with an avereage size of 4.89 per household.

Education:

Pre-school:

Pre-school at the barangay were served by the Day Care Center with an enrollment of an ideal number of 10 children for S/Y 2007-2008. The temporary and present site of the Day Care Center is the Barangay hall since the old building in the barangay where the Day Care Center formerly housed was totally damaged by the typhoon reming during the occurrence last November, 2006, hence it was unfit for the children to use the edifice.

Elementary:

Elementary school children at the barangay was primarily served by the Canaman Central School who offeres complete elementary grade 1-6. At present, it was severd by a highly competent 24 regular permanent teachers, 3 locally funded out of the Special Education Fund or SEF.

Secondary:

For their Secondary education, most of the parent send their student at Sta.Cruz High School, a purely secondary public school located at the Barangay Sta.Cruz, Canaman, Camarines Sur which is half kilometer away from Barangay Dinaga.

Tertiary:

It also well noted that few high school graduates did not pursue their tertiary studies. this can be attributed to the long-standing reason of poverty. Some high school graduate opted to be a working student just to continue their studies and reach their goal in life and some are working to augment the meager income of their parents to support the families.

Health and Social Services:

Barangay Dinaga has four community-based health workers or the Barangay Health Workers and their skilss includes health education, conducting survey, OPT, BP taking and giving lecture on family planning. They also actively participating in seminar and trainings conducted by some concered agencies to enhance their capabilities in delivering basic health service to the constituents of Barangay Dinaga.

Land Area and Utilization Pattern:

Barangay Dinaga has an aggregate area of 58.585 hectares, and 54.05 or 92.26% od which is devoted to rice production. The remaining portion was for the built-up areas, such as residential and instututional areas utilizing 2.5 hectares.

Geographical Location:

Barangay Dinaga, is strategically located in Canaman, Camarines Sur. It is bounded on the North by Brgy.Sta.Cru; on the south by Del Rosario and San Vicente; on the East by brgys. Haring, Agustin and Baras; and on the west by bry.Pangpang.

Mission

The Barangay Dinaga shall promote and enhace the quality of life of its poeple by providing equal opportunities for socio-cultural-economic and political development, through honest and effective governance with activve multi-sectoral participation and sustainable economic balance.

Vision

The Barangay of Dinaga will be a progressive community. A good, loving, peaceful, and healthy environment; the standard of education; with quality nutrition and health services for all.

History

Dinaga is another of the five barangays that constitutes the poblacion of Canaman. It is bounded on the north by the canaman Creek and the Barangay Sta.Cruz. Its southern side is a flat stretch of rice land that reach all way to Barangay San Vicente and Del Rosario. Dinaga's Eastern part shares its boundary with Baras, San Austin and Haring, while to its west is the Barangay of Pangpang, the common boundary of which runs straight down the middle of the town's catholic church.

It may be accurately said that Dinaga is spin-off of Barangay Pangpang, and that evidently came into official being during Spanish times perhaps during the part of the 1600. Before that, the area was low, marshy place which went under water when the high tide from San Miguel Bay caused the water the Bicol River to back up and prevent the creek from emptying into it. These daily tidal floods were worsened whenever it rained. Thus, the poeple had no desire to live in the swampy place.

Oral history says that before the poeple moved into low laying area, they patiently dumped into the place in order to keep the daily tidal flood away from their new homesites. Obviously , the work most likely done by a combination of community hongloan and corvee labor or polo-strecthed overtime, with the inhabitants first building up the site where they had settled in, on the western portion of the present barangay, immediately beside the church. Later on, as more poeple settled the place which later on becaome part of the central settlement(casco de la poblacion), they dumped more earth fill on both sides of the trail leading towards the cementery, the parallel earthfill just wide enough to build house on. Thus, the raised portion of Dinaga is axe-shaped, with its western end the head and its eastern side the handle. It also helps explain why houses on its eastern portion are laid in panel pattern, as against the grid pattern in the western side as well as in adjacent Pangpang.

Logically too, the poeple originally refered to place as DINAGAAN(filled with earth). Later, usage shortened the referential name to Dinaga, and it's the name that the barangay carries some three centuries later, although for a while in the early 1900s, it went the name "BAGONG BAYAN".
