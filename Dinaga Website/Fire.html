What to do in case of

Fire Emergency

Afire emergencyis any unplanned event that involves fire, threatening life, property, and the environment. It can range from a small, contained fire to a large, uncontrollable blaze. Immediate action is required to prevent injury, loss of life, and significant damage.

IN CASE OF FIRE

  1. Stay calm and evacuate immediately.
  2. Sound the fire alarm and call emergency hotlines for assistance.
  3. If youâ€™re in a building, use the stairs and do not use elevators.
  4. Before opening doors, check the handle first. If hot, DO NOT OPEN as the room may already be on fire.
  5. If there is smoke, get down low and cover your mouth and nose with a damp cloth.
  6. If your clothes catch fire, STOP running, DROP on the floor, and ROLL to put out the flames.
  7. If trapped, stay in the room and signal for help using bright-colored cloth at the window or make noise.
  8. After the fire, do not return inside your house or building unless authorities deem it safe.
  9. Have yourself checked for injuries.
