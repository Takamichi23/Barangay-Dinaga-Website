How to Apply for Barangay Document

Barangay documents certify your identity, residency, or financial status. These are often required for job applications, government permits, scholarships, and legal purposes.

> Step 1 – Prepare the Requirements
  1. Government-issued ID (e.g., Passport, Driver's License, Voter’s ID).
  2. Proof of residence (e.g., utility bill, lease agreement).
  3. Recent 1x1 or 2x2 photo (optional, depending on the barangay).
  4. Processing fee (amount varies per barangay).
> Step 2 – Visit the Barangay Hall
  1. Go to your local barangay office during operating hours.
  2. Inform the staff about the specific document you need.
> Step 3 – Complete the Application Form
  1. Fill out the form provided by the barangay office.
  2. Clearly state the purpose for requesting the document.
> Step 4 – Submit the Requirements
  1. Hand in your completed form and supporting documents.
  2. Pay the processing fee and secure the receipt.
> Step 5 – Wait for Processing
  1. Processing usually takes a few minutes to a couple of hours.
  2. Some documents may need approval from the Barangay Captain.
> Step 6 – Claim your Barangay Document
  1. Retrieve your document from the barangay office.
  2. Verify that all details are correct before leaving.
> What documents can I apply for?

Find answers to common questions about applying for barangay-issued documents. Learn more about the application process, requirements, and processing times.

> How long does it take to process my application?

Processing times vary depending on the barangay, but most applications are processed within the same day.

> Is there a fee for barangay documents?

Yes, fees vary by barangay and type of document. Check with your barangay hall for specific costs.

> Can I apply for someone else?

Yes, but you may need an authorization letter and additional documentation.

> How will I know when my document is ready?

You will be notified by the barangay office once your document is ready for pickup.

> What should I do if I made a mistake on my application?

If there’s a mistake on your application, contact your barangay office immediately to correct the error.
