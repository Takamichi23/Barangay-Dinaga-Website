Citizen's Guides

What to do in case of

Earthquake


BEFORE

  1. Know the hazards in your area.
  2. Familiarize yourself with the following:
    • Fire extinguishers (Mga pang-apula ng apoy)
    • Medical kit (Mga gamit pang-medikal)
    • Exit routes (Ligtas na daanan palabas ng gusali)
    • Evacuation plan (Plano sa paglikas ng mga tao)
  3. Check your house and have it repaired if necessary.
  4. Store harmful chemicals and flammable materials properly.
  5. Secure heavy furniture and hanging objects.
  6. Prepare your family’s Emergency Go Bag containing items needed for survival.
  7. Participate in office and community earthquake drills.

DURING

WHEN INSIDE A BUILDING "DUCK, COVER, AND HOLD":

  1. Duck under a strong table and hold on to it. Stay alert for potential threats.
  2. Stay away from glass windows, shelves, and heavy objects.
  3. After the shaking stops, exit the building and go to the designated evacuation area.

WHEN YOU ARE OUTSIDE:

  1. Move to an open area.
  2. Stay away from buildings, trees, electric posts, and landslide-prone areas.
  3. If you’re in a moving vehicle, stop and exit the vehicle.

AFTER

  1. Stay alert for aftershocks.
  2. Assess yourself and others for injuries. Provide first aid if necessary.
  3. Prioritize the needs of older persons, pregnant women, PWDs, and children.
  4. If living in a coastal area and there is a threat of a tsunami, evacuate to higher ground immediately.
  5. Check for spills of toxic and flammable chemicals.
  6. Wait for authorities to declare that it is safe to return.
  7. Check for damages in water and LPG leaks.
