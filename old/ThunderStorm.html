What to do in a

Thunderstorm

Thunderstormsare powerful weather phenomena that bring heavy rain, strong winds, lightning, and thunder. These storms can lead to power outages, flooding, and lightning strikes.

BEFORE

  1. Monitor weather updates and warnings through reliable sources.
  2. Secure outdoor items that could become hazardous during strong winds.
  3. Prepare an emergency kit with essentials such as flashlights, batteries, and first aid supplies.
  4. Unplug appliances and devices to protect them from power surges.
  5. Stay indoors and avoid open spaces during a thunderstorm warning.

DURING

  1. Stay away from windows, doors, and metal objects.
  2. Avoid using electrical appliances or wired phones.
  3. If outside, seek shelter in a car or a sturdy building. Avoid tall objects like trees and poles.
  4. Do not take shelter under a tree or in open fields.
  5. Avoid water-related activities, including swimming or bathing.

AFTER

  1. Wait for an official announcement before leaving your shelter.
  2. Be cautious of fallen power lines and report them to authorities.
  3. Inspect your property for damage and address hazards such as broken glass or debris.
  4. Assist neighbors or others in need of help, but ensure personal safety first.
  5. Continue monitoring news for updates and information on any potential hazards.
