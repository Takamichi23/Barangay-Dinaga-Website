Tips to prevent

Heat Stroke

Preventing heat stroke,especially in hot weather or during physical activities, involves taking proactive steps to keep your body cool and hydrated. Here are some key measures to prevent heat stroke:

  1. Stay Hydrated
    • Drink plenty of water: Even if you don’t feel thirsty, drink fluids regularly, especially during physical activity or when it’s hot outside.
    • Avoid caffeine and alcohol: These can dehydrate your body.
    • Electrolyte drinks: During intense activities, replenish electrolytes lost through sweat.
  2. Wear Appropriate Clothing
    • Lightweight and loose-fitting: Opt for breathable, loose clothes that allow heat to escape.
    • Light-colored clothing: Dark colors absorb more heat, while lighter colors reflect it.
    • Hats and sunglasses: Wear a wide-brimmed hat and sunglasses to protect yourself from direct sun exposure.
  3. Seek Shade and Cool Areas
    • Avoid direct sun exposure: Stay in shaded or cool areas during peak heat times, typically from 10 AM to 4 PM.
    • Use fans or air conditioning: If indoors, ensure proper ventilation or use cooling systems.
    • Plan outdoor activities early or late: Schedule physical activities during cooler parts of the day.
  4. Take Regular Breaks
    • Rest frequently when outdoors, especially during physical exertion, to allow your body to cool down.
    • Use wet towels or cooling devices on your neck and wrists during breaks to help reduce body temperature.
  5. Acclimatize to Heat Gradually
    • If you are not used to hot climates, gradually increase your exposure and intensity of activities over several days to allow your body to adjust.
