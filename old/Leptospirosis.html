Tips to Prevent

Leptospirosis

Leptospirosisis a bacterial infection transmitted primarily through water contaminated by the urine of infected animals, particularly rats. Preventing leptospirosis involves minimizing exposure to contaminated water and practicing good hygiene. Here are key tips to help prevent leptospirosis:

1. Avoid Wading in Floodwaters

  • Stay away from flooded areas: Leptospirosis is often contracted through contact with floodwaters, which can be contaminated by animal urine.
  • Use protective gear: If contact with floodwater is unavoidable, wear waterproof boots and gloves to minimize exposure, especially if you have cuts or open wounds.

2. Maintain Proper Sanitation

  • Eliminate rodents: Control rat populations in your home and workplace by properly disposing of food waste and keeping living spaces clean.
  • Seal food properly: Store food in rodent-proof containers to prevent contamination by animals.
  • Fix leaks and drainage: Ensure proper drainage and repair any leaks to prevent water accumulation, which could attract rodents.

3. Practice Good Hygiene

  • Wash hands and skin after contact: If you come into contact with potentially contaminated water, soil, or animals, thoroughly wash your hands and clean the skin properly after any possible exposure.
  • Clean wounds immediately: If you have any cuts or abrasions, keep them clean and covered to prevent infection.

4. Wear Protective Clothing in High-Risk Areas

  • Wear protective clothing: In areas with frequent floods or high exposure to animal waste, wear appropriate protective gear such as boots, gloves, and waterproof clothing.
  • Use antiseptics on skin after exposure: If contact with floodwater occurs, use antiseptic solutions to clean the skin, especially around cuts or abrasions.
